# Block Blast PvP — Prototype Design Doc

**Status:** Prototype / POC
**Target platform:** Mobile (portrait, one-handed)
**Engine assumption:** Unity (project already set up)
**Scope:** Single PvP match loop. No meta, no monetization, no progression. Prove the core is fun.

---

## 1. High Concept

A real-time, mobile-first, head-to-head puzzle game. Two players, two boards, drag-and-drop block pieces. Clear lines on your board to send garbage to the opponent's board. Lowest occupancy at the 90-second buzzer wins.

**One-line pitch:** *Block Blast meets competitive multiplayer, in 90 seconds, on your phone.*

**What this is NOT:**
- Not a Block Blast clone — has rotation, has a clock, has PvP.
- Not Tetris — pieces don't fall, 3-piece batch tray, 9×9 grid.
- Not single-player puzzle — every clear matters because it attacks an opponent.

---

## 2. Core Loop

```
Match start
  ↓
Both players receive identical 3-piece tray
  ↓
[Player turn — concurrent, real-time]
  Drag piece onto 9×9 board → snap to valid placement
  Cleared lines? → calculate attack → send garbage to opponent
  Receive garbage? → opponent's clears materialize on your board
  Repeat until tray is empty
  ↓
New 3-piece tray (mirrored from seeded queue)
  ↓
Loop until timer expires or one player tops out
  ↓
Resolve win condition → result screen
```

**Session length:** 90 seconds (hard cap).

---

## 3. Board & Pieces

### Board
- **Size:** 9×9 grid (81 cells)
- **Cell states:**
  - **Empty** — placeable
  - **Player cell** — placed by you, blocks placement, counts toward your attacks when cleared
  - **Garbage cell** — sent by the opponent, blocks placement, does NOT count toward your attacks, auto-decays over time (see §5)
- **Visual:** Sudoku-style subtle 3×3 sub-grid lines (helps spatial reading, no gameplay function). Garbage cells use a visually distinct fill (different color + subtle pulse) so the player can read board state instantly.

### Pieces
- Standard Block Blast / 1010!-style piece set, **with rotation enabled**.
- Each piece is 1–5 cells, polyomino shapes.
- Recommended starting piece pool (final tuning later):

| Category | Examples | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Singles & lines | 1×1, 1×2, 1×3, 1×4, 1×5 (+ vertical) | Common |
| Squares | 2×2, 3×3 | Medium |
| L / J / T / S / Z | All 4 rotations each | Medium |
| Plus shapes | + (5-cell cross) | Rare |
| Corners | 2×2 with one cell missing | Medium |

- **Total unique piece shapes:** ~18 (including rotation variants as a single entry — rotation is player-controlled).
- **Rotation:** Tap piece in tray to cycle through valid rotations (4 max). Or two-finger twist on board.

### Piece Tray
- **3 pieces visible** at any time, drawn from the queue.
- Player can place pieces in any order.
- When all 3 pieces are placed → new tray of 3 is drawn from the queue.
- **A piece cannot be skipped.** If no legal placement exists for any tray piece → player tops out.

### Queue (7-bag system)
- Both players share a **single seeded sequence** (mirrored queue) for the entire match.
- Queue is generated by shuffling all piece types in batches of 7 (Tetris-style bag randomization).
- Seed is determined at match start (e.g., timestamp + match ID) and is identical for both clients.
- **Why mirror:** eliminates "I got worse pieces" complaints. Pure skill expression.

---

## 4. Clearing Lines

### What clears
A row OR column is cleared when **all 9 cells in that row/column are occupied**.

### Simultaneous clears (combos)
If a single piece placement completes multiple rows and/or columns at once, they all clear in the same tick. This is a "combo" and triggers a combo bonus (see §5).

### No 3×3 sub-grid clears
Despite the Sudoku visual reference, 3×3 sub-grids do NOT clear. Only full rows and full columns. Keep it simple for the prototype.

---

## 5. Garbage / Attack System ("Echo Garbage")

**The defining mechanic. Read this section carefully.**

### Principle
> The garbage you send to your opponent is a mirror of the cells you just cleared, with player cells contributing at full value and cleared garbage cells contributing at a reduced rate.

This prevents a runaway snowball / ping-pong loop while still rewarding the skill of clearing through pressure. Garbage you clear gives you partial credit toward your counter-attack — you can't simply launder the opponent's pressure back at them at 100%, but clearing through their attacks isn't worthless either.

### Send formula
```
own_cells_in_clear     = number of YOUR-OWN cells in the rows/columns you cleared this tick
garbage_cells_in_clear = number of opponent-sent garbage cells in the same rows/columns
                         (note: row+column combo deduplicates intersection cells)

weighted_cells = own_cells_in_clear + (garbage_cells_in_clear × GARBAGE_CLEAR_CREDIT)
                 // GARBAGE_CLEAR_CREDIT default 0.30 — tunable in §9

base_send = weighted_cells × send_multiplier

// UI must clearly display the outgoing cell count above the clear animation
// (e.g., "+6 sent") so players understand why a garbage-heavy clear sent less.

send_multiplier =
  0.70  if 1 line cleared
  0.80  if 2 lines cleared simultaneously
  1.00  if 3 lines cleared simultaneously
  1.20  if 4+ lines cleared simultaneously

comeback_bonus = 1.30 if YOUR occupancy is > 60% at the moment of the clear, else 1.00

final_send = floor(base_send × comeback_bonus)
final_send = min(final_send, 12)   # hard cap to prevent one-shot kills
```

### Where garbage lands on opponent's board
- Mirror the **column index** of every cleared cell. (Optional, for cleaner mobile feel: drop all garbage cells to the lowest empty row of their respective columns — "gravity" garbage. Pick one for prototype, A/B later. **Prototype default: gravity.**)
- Garbage cells appear with a brief "incoming" animation (~0.3s) so the opponent can react and plan around them.
- If garbage causes the opponent to have no legal placement → they top out → instant loss.

### Garbage decay (defensive lifeline)
Garbage cells auto-expire over time, but with two safeguards to prevent passive / stalling play.

**Lifecycle of a single garbage cell:**
1. **Grace phase** (`GARBAGE_GRACE_SECONDS`, default **6s**): cell is fully solid, no fade, no decay progress.
2. **Fade phase** (`GARBAGE_FADE_SECONDS`, default **4s**): cell visibly fades / pulses and expires at end of phase.
3. Total lifetime: 10s. Slot returns to empty after expiry.

**Anti-stall safeguard — decay only ticks while the player is actively engaged:**
- If the player has not placed a piece in the last `STALL_THRESHOLD_SECONDS` (default **2s**), decay timers on their board PAUSE.
- This prevents the "stop placing → wait for garbage to fade" exploit. You must keep playing for relief to arrive.
- Timer resumes immediately on next valid placement.

**Other rules:**
- **No timer reset.** New garbage landing in the same column above existing garbage doesn't refresh anyone's timer — each cell decays on its own schedule.
- **Cleared garbage clears normally.** If you complete a full row/column that contains garbage cells, those garbage cells clear with the line — timers are moot at that point. As stated in the principle above, they contribute to your attack at the `GARBAGE_CLEAR_CREDIT` rate, not at full value.

**Why decay exists:** without it, defense is purely "out-clear your opponent" — favors aggressors, creates death spirals. Decay gives defenders a survival option: weather the wave, recover board space. Aggression still wins on the scoreboard (you'll be behind on cells), but you're not auto-dead from one big combo.

**Why the safeguards:** plain "10s and gone" rewards stalling — a losing player can place into safe corners and wait out the opponent's pressure. Grace phase ensures attacks land *meaningfully* before relief begins. Stall pause ensures relief requires engagement. Together they make decay a defensive *option*, not a defensive *exploit*.

**End-of-match note:** because decay can clear garbage cells in the final seconds, the last ~10s of a match has reduced strategic value for attackers. This is by design (mobile pacing) but worth watching in playtests — if matches consistently end with both boards near-empty, consider scaling decay down (e.g., halving timers in the final 15s) in a future iteration.

### Visual
- Cleared cells "lift off" the sender's board.
- Travel across a center divider with a trail.
- Land on the opponent's board with a thud + shake.
- This sequence is the **core fantasy** — it must feel impactful.

## 6. Win Conditions

In priority order:

1. **Top out (instant win):** if at any point in the match, a player has no legal placement for any of their 3 tray pieces, the opponent wins immediately.
2. **Timer expiry (90 seconds):** whoever has **fewer occupied cells** on their board at buzzer wins. Both player cells AND active garbage cells count toward occupancy (a garbage cell on your board at buzzer is still in your way — it's part of your problem).
3. **Tiebreaker:** if occupancy is equal at buzzer → most lines cleared during the match wins.
4. **Final tiebreaker:** if still tied → draw (acceptable for prototype).

---

## 7. Match Flow

### Pre-match
- Matchmaking screen (for prototype: instant local match, bot opponent or hot-seat 2-player on one device).
- Both players' boards initialize empty.
- 3-2-1 countdown.

### Match
- Both boards visible simultaneously on screen:
  - **Portrait layout:**
    - Top half: opponent's board (smaller, ~40% screen height)
    - Bottom half: player's board + piece tray (larger, ~60% screen height)
  - Timer in center.
  - Lines-cleared counter for each player visible.
- Concurrent real-time play. No turns.

### End
- Result screen: WIN / LOSS / DRAW + stats:
  - Final occupancy (yours vs opponent)
  - Lines cleared
  - Cells sent
  - Biggest combo
- Single "Play again" button. No meta, no rewards in prototype.

## 8. Controls (Mobile, Portrait)

- **Drag piece from tray onto board** with thumb.
- **Tap piece in tray** to rotate (cycles through valid rotations).
- **Long-press piece** to see a "ghost" preview on the board before committing.
- **Release** to place. If invalid → piece snaps back to tray.

One-handed play is a hard requirement. Test with both left- and right-thumb users.

## 9. Tuning Constants (Single Source of Truth)

All tunable values in one ScriptableObject (Unity convention). Designer can change without code edits.

```
BOARD_WIDTH                  = 9
BOARD_HEIGHT                 = 9
PIECE_TRAY_SIZE              = 3
MATCH_DURATION_SECONDS       = 90
COUNTDOWN_SECONDS            = 3

SEND_MULT_1_LINE             = 0.70
SEND_MULT_2_LINES            = 0.80
SEND_MULT_3_LINES            = 1.00
SEND_MULT_4_PLUS_LINES       = 1.20

COMEBACK_OCCUPANCY_THRESHOLD = 0.60
COMEBACK_MULTIPLIER          = 1.30

MAX_CELLS_PER_ATTACK         = 12

GARBAGE_TRAVEL_TIME_SECONDS  = 0.30
GARBAGE_GRAVITY              = true   // false = mirror column position exactly

GARBAGE_GRACE_SECONDS        = 6.0    // full-pressure phase before fade begins
GARBAGE_FADE_SECONDS         = 4.0    // visible fade phase, ends in expiry
STALL_THRESHOLD_SECONDS      = 2.0    // no placement for this long → decay pauses
GARBAGE_CLEAR_CREDIT         = 0.30   // attack contribution per garbage cell cleared (0 = none, 1 = full credit)
```

## 10. AI Opponent (Prototype Only)

For prototype testing without networking:

- **Bot difficulty levels:** Easy, Medium, Hard.
- Bot reads its own board state, evaluates placements with a simple heuristic:
  - Maximize lines cleared
  - Minimize new occupancy
  - Slight randomness for "personality"
- **Bot reaction time:** 0.5–2.0s per piece placement (Easy = slow, Hard = fast).
- Bot uses the SAME piece queue as the player (mirrored).

This lets us test feel without needing a netcode layer in week 1.

## 11. What's NOT in the Prototype

Explicitly out of scope. Do not implement:

- ❌ Multiplayer netcode / matchmaking servers
- ❌ User accounts, login, profiles
- ❌ Cosmetics, skins, themes
- ❌ Battle pass, season pass, daily rewards
- ❌ Currency, shop, IAP
- ❌ Ranked, leagues, ELO
- ❌ Tutorial / onboarding (a 5-second tooltip is enough)
- ❌ Sound design beyond placeholder SFX (place / clear / send / receive / win / lose)
- ❌ Music
- ❌ Particle FX beyond minimal placement + clear flash
- ❌ Settings menu
- ❌ Analytics (add later when prototype validates)

**The prototype proves ONE thing:** is the moment-to-moment match fun? Everything else is noise.

## 12. Success Criteria for Prototype

The prototype is "good enough" to move forward when:

1. A new player understands the rules within 30 seconds of seeing it.
2. A match consistently lasts ~60–90 seconds.
3. Both top-out wins AND timer wins occur in roughly equal proportions in playtests (neither dominates).
4. Internal playtesters describe the moment of sending a big garbage attack as "satisfying."
5. Comeback mechanic actually produces comebacks (>15% of matches where the trailing player wins).
6. No "feels like I lost because of luck" complaints from playtesters of similar skill.

If 4 of 6 are met → prototype validates. Iterate or move to next phase.

## 13. Recommended Implementation Order

For the implementing engineer/agent:

1. **Board & piece data model** — 9×9 grid, piece definitions, placement validation, line clear detection.
2. **Solo placement loop** — drag pieces from tray, place on board, clear lines. No PvP yet. Make this *feel good* first.
3. **Two-board side-by-side** — duplicate the solo loop for two boards on screen. Still no interaction between them.
4. **Echo garbage system** — wire clears on board A to spawn garbage on board B (and vice versa). This is the magic moment.
5. **Win conditions** — timer, top-out detection, result screen.
6. **Bot AI** — simple heuristic opponent.
7. **Tuning pass** — playtest, adjust constants in §9.

Do NOT build matchmaking, accounts, or netcode in the prototype. Bot is sufficient.

## 15. Glossary

- **Echo garbage:** the attack system where cleared cells are mirrored onto the opponent's board.
- **Garbage decay:** garbage cells auto-vanish after a fixed duration (10s default), giving defenders a "survive the wave" option.
- **Cell ownership:** every occupied cell is tagged either as a player cell (yours) or a garbage cell (sent by the opponent). Ownership affects attack contribution.
- **7-bag:** randomization scheme that shuffles all piece types in batches of 7, guaranteeing variety.
- **Top out:** when no piece in the tray has a legal placement on the board. Triggers instant loss.
- **Occupancy:** percentage of cells filled on a board (occupied cells ÷ 81).
- **Combo:** clearing 2+ rows/columns with a single piece placement.
- **Tray:** the slot holding the 3 pieces available for placement.
