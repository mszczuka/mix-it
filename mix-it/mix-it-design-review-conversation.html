Definitions used in this review

  • Aligned (🟢) — matches the archetype's proven pattern 1:1 or with cosmetic deviation only.
  • Hybrid (🟡) — partial match; deviates in a way that is not clearly cosmetic and not clearly considered innovation.
  • Innovation – plugin (🟣) — deliberate new/different element in the moment-to-moment core. Correct place for innovation.
  • Innovation – archetype (🔴) — new/different element inside the meta/archetype shell. Framework-flagged as risky.
  • Missing (⚫) — archetype reference has this, design does not.
  • Paused / Deferred (⏸) — consciously deferred to later phase.
  • Problematic (⚠️) — conflicts with the archetype's motivations or with the time-scale contract.
  • Unknown (❔) — design does not state enough to classify.

1. Framing

  • Working title: mix-it
  • Designer-declared target archetype: Casual PvP Arena / Match-Masters-style asynchronous 1v1 arena. The meta layer is explicitly refactored (v7.0–v7.2) to copy MM 1:1 — single-soft-currency, Booster Stakes Multiplier, auto-tier arena, sticker collection, pass, chests.
  • Plugin: a real-time parallel-board water-sort + serve-race. Two players on the same scrambled pour-sort board pour colors into glasses, fill glasses purely, then race to serve a shared 3-customer queue in the middle. 90-second match; Color Spawn recharges on timer; combo window 10s; first-serve bonus + 2s grace window.
  • Scope of review: v7.2 state as current truth, walking backwards into v7.1/v7.0/v6 for deeper systems.
  • Unknown at review time: explicit session-length target, FTUE / tutorial spec, early-win-rate target, match-length fidelity.

10. Reviewer opinion

Mix-it is on track, meta-side. The v7.0 → v7.2 arc is rare: three consecutive patches whose purpose is to remove meta-layer invention and collapse to a proven leader's shape. That's strategically correct.

The plugin is the real question, and it is unresolved. The water-sort + parallel-board + shared-customer-race + combo-window loop is genuinely novel. That's the correct place for innovation. But no retention evidence is cited in the docs, and no FTUE spec exists for a first-minute with unusually high cognitive load. If the plugin does not independently retain, the shell cannot save it.

Weakest layer: the week loop. Bar Pass covers month; Lucky Box + dailies cover day; plugin covers seconds and minutes; but "what am I climbing this week?" is unanswered for the KotH cohort.

Single most important question: Does the plugin (parallel-board, shared-queue, 90s) retain on its own? Everything else is secondary.

Verdicts on 5 Proposed Modifications

1. Mixable liquid layers ✅ Good idea — plugin-layer, right place

What it does: Adds a second rule to a tight rule-set (water-sort).

Verdict: Keep it, but as a feature of specific colors or events, not a universal rule.

Pros:

  • Adds tactical axis — "do I pour now or wait for the second mixable to merge?"
  • Creates new booster vectors: "Mark top layer as mixable," "Consolidate all mixables"
  • Expands skill ceiling without changing the archetype

Watch-outs:

  • Plugin is already cognitively loaded. FTUE must teach it in a separate beat.
  • Rule: if a player can't explain "mixable" in one sentence after their second match, it's too much.
  • Visual legibility critical — mixable needs an instantly-readable shader (bubbles? stripes? glow?).

Tag: 🟣 Plugin innovation — framework-correct place.

2. Thick liquid (pour anywhere) ✅ Yes, but as a rare resource — not a baseline color

What it does: Breaks the fundamental water-sort constraint (top-layer-match or empty).

Verdict: Too powerful to be a regular color. But excellent as:

  • A booster ("Thickener" — turns your next pour into thick)
  • A Color Spawn rare drop (1-in-N spawns is thick)
  • A Gold-tier stake reward

Why not baseline: Water-sort is fun because the constraint is tight. Remove the constraint for one color and every strategic decision collapses to "use the thick first." Dominant strategy kills it.

Why as a booster: Match Masters uses exactly this pattern — "special" pieces that break normal match-3 rules, gated behind boosters. Scales with Stakes Multiplier naturally.

Tag: 🟣 Plugin innovation, scoped as booster/rare.

3. Discovery / PvE drink collection ⚠️ I have reservations — hold this one

The concern: You just finished v7.0 → v7.2 aggressively removing archetype-layer invention to become MM-faithful. MM is PvP-only. 8BP has Practice Mode but it's a training ground, not a collection meta. A PvE discovery mode is a different archetype's feature — it belongs to merge games, cooking games, or idle/collection games.

The risk:

  • Attention split. Every PvE minute is a PvP minute not played. Your retention math, monetization, and matchmaking pool all assume PvP-primary.
  • Recipe collection competes with Sticker collection. A second collection system dilutes both.
  • Scope. Recipe database, combination detection, drink art per combo, naming, UI, gates — a second game.

When it could work:

  • As a tutorial sandbox / first-session mode — new players mix freely for 2–3 minutes before PvP. Solves FTUE gap, doesn't compete long-term.
  • As a daily challenge — "make a drink with exactly 3 colors today" → small coin reward.
  • As a feature of the Venue — each building unlocks a signature drink recipe. Ties PvE into existing meta instead of creating a parallel one.

What I'd disagree with: Shipping it as a full standalone mode in MVP. Two games' worth of scope.

Tag: 🔴 Archetype-layer innovation — framework-flagged as risky. Reopen only if it serves FTUE or folds into Venue.

What would change my mind: Playtest data showing new players bounce in the first 3 matches of full PvP. Then PvE sandbox as FTUE becomes a solution to a measured problem.

4. Guild PvP (Football Rivals-style real-time team match) ✅ Strong idea — but phasing matters

What it does: Solves TWO of the biggest flagged gaps — the week-scale King-of-the-Hill loop and the social/teams layer.

Pros:

  • Football Rivals' guild-match pattern (serve drinks → contribute to guild score → real-time tally) is basically Clash Royale Clan Wars adapted for casual-skill genre. Proven pattern.
  • Natural fit for Mix-It plugin: "serve X drinks in the next 48h to fill the guild's Gala meter" reads instantly.
  • Fills the week peak currently missing.
  • Drives session frequency — guild obligation is the stickiest retention lever in the genre.

But — phase-2, not MVP. Reasons:

  1. Needs Teams system first (currently ⏸ deferred). No guild wars without guilds.
  2. Real-time guild infrastructure is heavy tech — scoreboards, server-side aggregation, timers, anti-cheat. Clash Royale took 2 years post-launch to ship Clan Wars.
  3. Matchmaking a guild match requires a large active population — doesn't work pre-scale.

Suggested phasing:

  • MVP: solo game, stickers, Bar Pass
  • Post-MVP v1: Teams + team gifts (MM-pattern) + team chat
  • Post-MVP v2: Guild Match / Guild Gala (Football Rivals pattern)

Tag: 🟢 Archetype-aligned, ⏸ phase correctly.

5. Social casino — watch 3 bottles, bet on color combo ❌ I'd drop this

Direct opinion: Bad idea for Mix-It.

Structural problems:

  1. It's literally a slot machine — 3 random bottles, win on specific combos. That's the textbook slot-machine loop (3 reels, line matches, reveal, payout). Mix-It's positioning is "casual skill arena" — slot mechanics are a different archetype (SG / Coin Master lineage) with different audience, CPI math, LTV model, regulatory posture.
  2. App store / regulatory risk. Watching random reels for prize payouts approaches social-casino territory. Apple and Google both scrutinize this in non-casino-positioned apps.
  3. Breaks skill positioning. Your plugin is Skill Master + Thrill Seeker + Thinker food. A zero-skill passive gamble trains the opposite behavior and attracts a different cohort than your arena matchmaking needs.
  4. Monetization cannibalization. If players passively win via bottle spin, they play fewer matches. Your entire economy is tuned to match-count — breaking that axis breaks the whole model.
  5. Not in the archetype. None of your reference games (MM, 8BP, GC, BC, TH) ships a social casino. Adding one is a 🔴 archetype innovation without an evidence base.

What would change my mind:

  • If this is meant as a "free spin daily" with cosmetic-only rewards (frame, avatar, emoji) — it's a lightweight re-engagement hook, not a gambling loop. Same pattern as MM's lucky wheel. That's fine.

Tag: 🔴 Archetype innovation in the wrong direction. Recommend drop, or neuter to "daily cosmetic wheel" (genre-standard).
